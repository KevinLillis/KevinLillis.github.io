HW 07 — Credit Card Check

When making an on-line purchase, if you accidentally enter your credit card number wrong (say by swapping adjacent digits, like 43 for 34, or getting pairs of the same digit wrong, so 899 becomes 889) then someone else could end up being charged for your purchase. To help prevent these kinds of simple typing errors, credit card numbers are self checking. This means a simple check can be preformed to see whether or not the card number entered is valid.

Suppose we have the following Visa number (no, this is not a real credit card number)

4000 1234 5678 9314

We are interested in looking at every other number, so I'll color them differently, using red and blue:

4000  1234  5678  9314

  1. First we take every other digit in the number, going from left to right, starting with the first.
    In the example, these are: 4, 0, 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 1
  2. Now double each of these numbers.
    This would give: 8 , 0 , 2 , 6 , 10 , 14 , 18 , 2
  3. If any numbers from the previous step are greater than 9 (like 10 , 14 , 18 ), subtract 9 from that number (giving 1 , 5 , 9 ).
    Now the list of "doubled" numbers is: 8 , 0 , 2 , 6 , 1 , 5 , 9 , 2
  4. Now we add all of the "doubled" numbers together with all of the other numbers not looked at in step 1 ( 0, 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 3, 4 ).
    8 + 0 + 0 + 0 + 2 + 2 + 6 + 4 + 1 + 6 + 5 + 8 + 9 + 3 + 2 + 4 = 60
  5. If the sum is evenly divisible by 10, then the credit card number is valid. But if the sum is not divisible by 10, the number is invalid.
    Since 60 is evenly divisible by 10, 4000 1234 5678 9314 is a valid credit card number.

Assignment

Write a Python program that prompts the user to enter a credit card number and then displays whether the entry is valid or invalid. For this assignment a valid entry is any string that contains exactly 16 digits and those digits pass the check described above. Any non-digit characters entered by the user are ignored.
