Homework 03 — Split the Bill

Scenario

You and a few coworkers go out to lunch and agree to split the bill evenly. Here is how you decided to figure it out. There's the cost of the food. Then there's tax and a tip. The tax is figured just on cost of the food. Likewise, the tip is figured just on the cost of the food. Next you add up the cost of the food, the amount of the tax, and the amount of the tip. Finally, you divide that total by the number of people at the table. Here is a spreadsheet that does the calculation.

Assignment

Write a program that prompts for the following pieces of information:

  • Cost of the food
  • Tax rate (percent)
  • Tip (percent)
  • Number of people

The program then displays how much each person owes.
Hint: Use the spreadsheet to check that your program is working correctly. Check thinks like: (i) There's only one person at lunch, (ii) There is no tax, (iii) The service is so poor you decide not to leave a tip. Play around and make sure you program is working correctly for different scenarios.

Sample Run

Below is an example of how your program should look when it runs. The things that the user types in are shown in green. You program does not need to use any colors. Other than that though, your program should look like the sample run, including the way the final dolar amount is formatted. 



     Cost of the food: 122.30
     Tax rate (percent): 3.4
     Tip (percent): 15
     Number of people: 5
     Each person owes $28.96
