Research Paper

Requirements

Topic

Your paper will be a review and discussion of some recent topic in information assurance. The topics are not confined to the course syllabus or topics addressed in the textbook. Your paper must directly discuss (and reference) at least three related scholarly articles published within the last five years from reputable technical referred journals or proceedings from related conferences. Note that books and popular magazines do not satisfy this requirement. All topics must be approved.

Grading

Because of the importance of clarity in the presentation of information, 20% of your grade will be based on how you wrote your paper, with the remaining 80% based on what you wrote. When determining the style component of your grade things such as spelling, punctuation, and grammar, as well as the overall readability of your paper will be considered. Plagiarism will not be tolerated. Failure to properly cite sources will significantly lower your grade, possible resulting in a grade of zero for the research paper.

Detailed Requirements

Your paper should be 5-7 pages long, not including the cover page and bibliography. It must be double spaced and use Times New Roman 12-point font with 1" margins on all sides. The pages must be numbered. Your paper should be organized as follows:

Cover Page

  • The title of the paper
  • Your name
  • The number and name of the class
  • The semester and year

Introduction

  • Give an overview of your subject
  • Define any key terminology needed to understand the topic
  • Highlight any background information on the topic needed to understand the direction of the paper
  • The introduction must end with a thesis statement that:
    • Tells what the overall paper will focus on
    • Briefly outlines the main points of the paper

Body

  • Clearly present the main points of the paper as listed in the thesis
  • Give strong examples, details, and explanations to support each main point
  • Be sure to use strong evidence from your sources
  • Citations must be provided

Conclusion

  • Restate your thesis from the introduction in different words
  • Briefly summarize each main point found in the body of the paper (avoid going over two sentences for each point)
  • End with an appropriate, meaningful sentence that ties the whole point of the paper together

Bibliography

The Bibliography often gives people problems. Minimally, a bibliography entry for a book should include the author(s), the title, the publisher, and the year of publication. When including a reference to a journal or magazine article, the volume and/or issue number should also be included. Any of the "standard" bibliography formats are acceptable; e.g., for a book, 

[BOND76] BONDY, J. A., and MURTY, U. S. R.. Graph Theory with Applications. North Holland, N.Y., 1976.

or for an article or paper 

[FORD56] FORD, L. R. Jr. and FULKERSON, D. R.. Maximal flow through a network. Canad. Jour. Math., 8 (1956), 399-404.

There are numerous technical writing textbooks available in the library which will give you more information on bibliographies.
