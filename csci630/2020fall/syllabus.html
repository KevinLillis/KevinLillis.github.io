Course Description

This course studies the issues related to management of the computer systems information security function in a corporate setting. Topics include the relationship of security to a corporate mission, creating security plans and policies, budget and implementation issues, risk assessment and management, and auditing and compliance.

Objectives

Upon completion of this course students will be able to:

  • Describe the role of the security plan in a corporate environment
  • Understand the issues related to federal mandates regarding information security
  • Demonstrate a risk assessment process
  • Be familiar with common security planning guidelines
  • Differentiate among: policy, procedure, and control as used in information security
  • Create a security audit planning document

Article Review Each student is to evaluate and analyze a current work of research in an area related information assurance. Please see the detailed requirements for the Article Review.

Research Paper Each student will prepare a paper that reviews and discusses of some recent topic in information assurance. The topics are not confined to the course syllabus or topics addressed in the textbook. The paper will discuss (and reference) at least three related scholarly articles published within the last five years from reputable technical referred journals or proceedings from related conferences. Please see the detailed requirements for the Research Paper.

Risk Assessment Project This project involves defining a fictional enterprise with various business functions. Students will identify information technology assets and functions to support the business needs of the enterprise. A qualitative and quantitative risk assessment for a subset of the IT assets will be conducted. Lastly a information security policy for employees of the enterprise will be developed. Below are the main activities for the Risk Assessment Project. Please see the detailed requirements for the Risk Assessment Project.

How to Succeed in this Class

Attend Class Showing up on time for every class is the easiest way to improve your grade — not merely because of the credit for participation, but also because of exposure to the content presented in class.

Level of Effort Normally a student can expect to spend a minimum of two hours of work, outside of class, for each credit unit. Thus a typical expected workload would be at least> six hours per week outside of class.

Prepare for Class Read all material ahead of time for each class.

Do the Homework Start working on the homework problems as soon as possible. Often, a problem that stumps you one day will seem obvious a day later. You should view the homework assignments as preparation for the exams. It is imperative that you understand and complete the homework.

Manage Your Time I encourage you to develop a system to actively manage you priorities and time. You should schedule time each week to complete assignments and to complete readings in advance of class. Effective time management will help you not only in this class but also in the rest of your studies and in your career. Poor time management and late completion of assignments will be detrimental to your learning experience and will be reflected in your grade for the class.

Stay Organized Organize your class notes and save all of your graded work. These will be a great resource when studying for the exams.

Visit Me If you have problems during the semester that affect your coursework, please see me as soon as possible so we can discuss your options for the course. If you find yourself falling behind in the class, please see me. It is likely we can work together to get you back on track.
